Mercury

Mercury is a rocky planet. It is mostyle composed of metals and rock.It's the smallest planet in our solar system with a diameter of 4,879 km. Mercury has no moons or rings.Named after Mercury, the messenger of the gods.Mercury is the most crated planet. The planet's surface is created due to constant encounters with asteroids and comets. Mercury has 38% the gravity of Earth.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and has no moons or rings. Named after the Roman goddess of love and bueaty, Venus. Has layers of clouds made up of sulphuric acids. Surface temperatures on Venus can go up to 471 degrees Celcius. Venus is the second birghtest object in the sky. This planet is called Earth's sister planet because it's size and mass is similar to our home planet. Also, both planets have a central core, a molten mantle and a crust.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and has two moons. It is the second smallest planet. Named after the Roman god of war. Mars is composed primarily of carbon dioxide. Mars has the tallest mountain in the solar system called Olympus Mons. Mars has dust storms that could last for months. Scientists have found traces of Martian atmosphere within meteorites on Earth.Water has been spotted on Mars.

Earth

Earth is the only planet with water on the surface. Earth is the third plant from the Sun. Earth has one moon. Our home planet is the largest of all terrestrial planets. This is the only planet not named after a Greek or Roman deity. So far, Earth is the only known planet to support life. It has a magnetic field caused by the nickel-iron core of the planet that protects the Earth from the effects of solar wind. The Earth is the densest planet with an average denisity of about 5.52 grams per cubic centimetre.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun. Jupiter has more than 75 moons. Jupiter is known as a "gas giant" because it's primarily made up of hydrogen and helium. This planet is the fourth brightest in the solar system. The only planets that are brighter than Jupiter are the Sun, Moon, and Venus. Jupiter is named after the king of the Roman gods. Jupiter orbits the Sun once every 11.8 Earth years. Jupiter's "Great Red Spot" is a region in the planet's atmosphere that produces an anticyclonic storm. Jupiter's moon Ganymede is larger than the planet Mercury.

Saturn

Saturn is the second largest planet and is the sixth planet from the Sun. Saturn is composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane. Due to the planet's low density and fast rotation, it is known as the flattest planet. Saturn has storms that are similar to Jupiter's. Saturn is primarily made up of hydrogen. Of the 150 moons, Saturn's largest moons are Titan and Rhea. Saturn is named after the Roman god Saturnus. Saturn is the fifth birghtest object in the solar system.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It was discovered by Sir William Herschel and was named after the ancient Greek deity Ouranos. Uranus makes a trip around the Sun once every 17 hours, 14 minutes. It is an "ice giant" with a hydrogen upper layer. The planet gets it's blue color from the water, ammonia, and methane crystals in its atmosphere. Uranus is the coldest planet in our solar system with minimum atmoshperic temperatures of -224 degrees Celcius. Uranus has eleven inner rings and two outer rings.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun. This planet was discovered on September 23rd 1846. Neptune was named after the Roman god of the sea. Neptune is a gas planet with clouds that take up to 18 hours to make one rotation. The planet is made of hydrogen, helium, and methane. The planet's blue appearance derives from the methane that absorbs red light.
